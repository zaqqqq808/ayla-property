Bali's property market has been one of the most-watched in Southeast Asia for half a decade. The question every investor asks in 2026: is the run sustainable, or are we late to the party?

The short answer is that Bali is still attractive — but the easy money is gone, and the dispersion between good and bad investments has widened dramatically.

## What changed in the last 24 months

Tourist arrivals have stabilised at around 6 million per year, up from the pre-2020 peak. New direct flights from Australia, Singapore, India and the Middle East have shifted demand patterns. Canggu and Seminyak rental yields have compressed as supply caught up; meanwhile, second-tier areas have seen yields hold or even expand.
